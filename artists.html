Our Programme

Curated. Not random.

The music programme at Wellbeing is run by Bar Director Marcus DeSouza and Creative Director Lara Mehta. It has its own budget, its own logic, and its own relationship with the city's music scene. We don't book big names to sell tickets. We book good artists because we like what they do. Sometimes those are the same thing.

Friday and Saturday nights have a headliner. Wednesday is jazz. Sunday afternoons are instrumental. Everything in between is what feels right that week.

Our Artists

Resident & Visiting

Afrobeats · Electronic · Amapiano

DJ Kemi

Kemi Okonkwo arrived in Mumbai in 2018 via Lagos, London, and a brief but formative stint in Cape Town. Her sets are generous — she plays for the room, not the recording. At Wellbeing she holds the Friday residency with a loyalty that has nothing to do with money and everything to do with the dancefloor's energy after midnight.

Her signature is patience. She builds slowly, reads the room, and the drop — when it comes — feels earned. Several producers have tried to bottle what she does. None have succeeded.

Fri, 9 May Fri, 23 May Fri, 6 Jun

Indie Folk · Carnatic Fusion · Live

Raahi

Vikram Nair plays under the name Raahi — which means "traveller" in Hindi, and he is. Trained in classical Carnatic music in Chennai, re-educated by a decade of indie folk in Edinburgh, and now back in Mumbai making music that belongs entirely to the intersection. He plays the rooftop most Saturday nights when the weather is kind.

His live setup is minimal: a guitar, a shruti box, and a loop pedal he has owned since 2011 and refuses to replace. Everything you hear is made in real time.

Sat, 10 May Sat, 24 May Sat, 7 Jun

Deep House · Minimal · Downtempo

Sable

Nobody knows Sable's real name. She prefers it that way. Her sets live in the late hours — after 1AM when the dinner crowd has thinned and the space belongs to people who chose to stay. The music is deep, deliberate, and occasionally uncomfortable in the best possible way.

She holds the Wellbeing late-night residency on the last Friday of every month. The set starts at 1AM. It ends when she decides it ends. We have never asked her to stop early. We are not going to start.

Fri, 30 May Fri, 27 Jun

Neo-Classical · Ambient · Piano

Zara Noor

Zara Noor is a pianist and composer who studied at the Royal Academy of Music and returned to Mumbai with a mission to make classical music feel alive to people who thought it was over their heads. It is not. Her Wednesday evening performances in The Lounge are the building's best-kept secret — and somehow still the hardest table to get on a weeknight.

She releases music sporadically and on her own terms. Her most recent album, Saudade Bandra, was recorded live at Wellbeing at 2AM with no audience. The room was perfect for it.

Wed, 7 May Wed, 14 May Wed, 21 May Wed, 28 May

House · Progressive · Electronic

DJ ATLAS

Aditya Sharma — DJ ATLAS — makes house music that sounds like it was composed specifically for large, dark rooms with excellent acoustics. He has played fabric in London, Fabric in Berlin, and Fabric in no other city because it's only in those two places. He brings that sensibility to Wellbeing without the attitude that sometimes accompanies it.

His sets on the Main Floor on the second Saturday of each month have built a following that is genuinely difficult to explain to someone who hasn't experienced it. Just come on a second Saturday. You'll understand by midnight.

Sat, 17 May Sat, 14 Jun

Jazz Piano · Live · Bebop · Contemporary

NINA K

Nina Krishnaswamy plays jazz piano the way good storytellers tell stories — with confidence, no wasted notes, and the occasional surprise that makes you rethink everything that came before it. She has been playing professionally since she was 17 and has the catalogue to prove it.

Her Sunday evening sets are acoustic, intimate, and available as a trio (with Roshan on bass and Tenzin on brushed drums) or solo depending on the night and her mood. The Lounge holds 60 people. Sunday evenings it feels like ten. This is a compliment.

Sun, 11 May Sun, 18 May Sun, 25 May

Hip-Hop · Neo-Soul · Live

VERB

Kabir Verma performs as VERB — and the name is intentional. His music is active, engaged, and lyrically specific in a way that makes you want to listen again after the first time. Raised in Dharavi, educated at Berklee Online, currently living in Bandra. The contradictions are features, not bugs.

His live shows at Wellbeing are part concert, part conversation. He talks between songs because he has things to say. The backing band (four pieces, always) is among the tightest in Mumbai. He performs on the first Saturday of each month on the Main Floor.

Sat, 3 May Sat, 7 Jun

Artist Bookings

Want an artist
for your event?

Our resident and visiting artists are available for private bookings through Wellbeing. We facilitate — the artist decides. If the occasion is interesting to them, they'll say yes. Fill out the form and we'll be in touch within 5 working days.

Artist fees start at ₹50,000 for a solo performance and scale with set length, band size, and date. We take a coordination fee of 15%, which covers logistics, rider management, and making sure everything actually happens as planned.
