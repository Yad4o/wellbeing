The Origin

Mumbai needed this.
We just built it.

Arjun Malhotra and Priya Sethna met at Soho House Mumbai's opening night in 2015. Both had spent years at private members clubs in London, Berlin, and New York. Both had the same conversation on the drive home: Mumbai deserves this, but it deserves a version that is actually Indian. Not imported. Not aspirationally foreign. Indian.

It took four years to find the right building, the right team, and the right conviction. The Loom Building — a 1940s textile warehouse in Bandra West, eight floors of exposed brick and industrial bones — became Wellbeing in October 2019. Six days before the world changed. They stayed open through all of it.

The Philosophy

Anti-VIP table.
Pro-experience.

We don't reserve the best table for the most famous person in the room. We give the best table to whoever booked it first. Fame is a distraction from the food. We like food more than fame.

The Building

A 1940s warehouse
reimagined.

The Loom Building was built in 1943 as part of Bandra's textile manufacturing district. When Arjun and Priya found it in 2017, it was a partially demolished shell — the floors were there, the bones were perfect, and the address was everything.

The renovation took 26 months. They kept everything structurally original — the brick, the columns, the ceiling height. They added everything experiential — the pool on the 8th floor, the rooftop on the 9th, the spa on the 7th. The result is a building that feels both very old and entirely new.

The People

The team that makes it real.

We don't hire for credentials. We hire for obsession. Everyone here is obsessed with something specific, and it shows.

Kavya Nair

Executive Chef

Grew up in Mangalore. Trained in Lyon at Institut Paul Bocuse. Returned to India with a mission: prove that coastal Indian food is one of the great cuisines of the world. The menu at Wellbeing is that proof.

Marcus DeSouza

Bar Director

Goan by birth, bartender by calling. Spent a decade in London at Artesian and Dukes before coming home. His cocktail menu has been described as "the most intelligent list in Mumbai" by Time Out. He'd say it's just honest.

Lara Mehta

Creative Director

Former art director at Vogue India, creative lead for three luxury hotel openings. Responsible for everything visual at Wellbeing — from the font on the menu to the way the light hits the bar at 9PM. Details are her religion.

Arjun Malhotra

Co-Founder

Former member of Soho House London, Berlin, and Mumbai. Spent 15 years in hospitality consulting before founding Wellbeing. Spends most evenings on the ground floor. Will talk to you about music for much longer than you expected.
